AHA POST-CARDIAC SURGERY DISCHARGE PROTOCOLS

AMERICAN HEART ASSOCIATION — POST-SURGICAL DISCHARGE CARE STANDARDS (2024 Update)

MEDICATION ADHERENCE PROTOCOL

Core Post-CABG Medication Regimen:
1. Aspirin 81mg daily — antiplatelet for graft patency; NEVER discontinue without physician approval
2. Beta-blocker (metoprolol/carvedilol) — heart rate/BP control; reduces MACE risk 25-35%
3. ACE inhibitor/ARB (lisinopril/valsartan) — cardiac remodeling prevention, essential if EF <40%
4. Statin (atorvastatin/rosuvastatin) — plaque stabilization, mandatory regardless of baseline LDL
5. Anticoagulation (warfarin/NOAC) — only if AF, mechanical valve, or thromboembolic indication

Medication Non-Adherence Risk Factors (screen at discharge):
- Lives alone / limited social support
- Cognitive impairment or literacy barriers
- Cost barriers (>4 medications, out-of-pocket burden)
- History of medication non-adherence
- Complex dosing schedules

WOUND CARE STANDARDS

Sternal Wound:
- Keep dry for 48 hours post-discharge
- Clean with mild soap, pat dry; no soaking (baths) until 4-6 weeks
- Apply no creams/ointments unless prescribed
- Cover with clean dressing until wound fully sealed (~10-14 days)

Leg Wound (SVG harvest site):
- Compression stockings for 4-6 weeks to reduce edema
- Expect bruising and numbness — normal
- Watch for: increased redness, warmth, drainage, fever >38°C — report immediately

ACTIVITY RESTRICTIONS BY WEEK

Week 1 (Days 1-7):
- Walking: 5-10 min, 3× daily; increase as tolerated
- No lifting >5 lbs
- No driving (reaction time impaired, cannot turn wheel with sternal restriction)
- No sexual activity

Week 2 (Days 8-14):
- Walking: 10-20 min, 3× daily; target 2,000 steps/day
- Light household tasks (cooking, light cleaning)
- Still no driving, no lifting >5-10 lbs
- Stairs: permitted with handrail support

Week 3-4 (Days 15-28):
- Walking: 20-30 min daily; 3,000-4,000 steps target
- May resume driving if approved by surgeon (typically 4-6 weeks post-op)
- Return to sedentary/desk work may be possible at Week 3-4 with physician clearance

Week 5-8:
- Cardiac rehabilitation referral — shown to reduce 30-day readmission by 30-50%
- Progressive aerobic activity
- Return to physically demanding work: 6-12 weeks depending on job requirements

DIETARY GUIDELINES POST-CARDIAC SURGERY
- Sodium: <2,000mg/day (especially if EF <40% or heart failure history)
- Fluid restriction: 1.5-2L/day if EF <35%
- Heart-healthy diet: Mediterranean or DASH pattern
- Alcohol: Maximum 1 drink/day for women, 2/day for men; none in first 30 days
- Potassium monitoring if on ACE inhibitor + diuretic (hyperkalemia risk)

FOLLOW-UP CARE SCHEDULE
- Surgeon wound check: 7-10 days post-discharge
- Cardiologist: 2-4 weeks post-discharge
- Primary care: 4-6 weeks post-discharge
- Cardiac rehabilitation: Referral within 21 days of discharge (Class I AHA recommendation)
- Lab work: BMP (potassium, creatinine, BUN) at 1-2 weeks if on diuretics or ACE inhibitor

EMERGENCY ACTION CRITERIA (Call 911 Immediately):
- Chest pain/pressure/tightness that doesn't resolve in 15 minutes
- Sudden severe shortness of breath at rest
- Loss of consciousness or near-syncope
- Signs of stroke: face drooping, arm weakness, speech difficulty (FAST protocol)
- Wound dehiscence (sternal wound opening)
- Fever >39°C (102°F) + wound redness/drainage

CONTACT CARE TEAM SAME DAY (Not Emergency, But Urgent):
- Fever 38-39°C (100.4-102°F)
- Increased wound drainage or redness
- Ankle swelling worse than yesterday
- Heart rate >100 for >2 hours
- SpO₂ <94% (if patient has oximeter)
- Unusual fatigue, can't complete activities they could yesterday
- Missed 2+ medication doses
