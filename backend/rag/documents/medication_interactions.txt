COMMON POST-CARDIAC SURGERY MEDICATIONS — Interactions & Monitoring Guide

METOPROLOL SUCCINATE (Beta-1 Selective Beta-blocker)
Mechanism: Reduces heart rate and myocardial oxygen demand; suppresses post-op arrhythmias
Standard Dose: 25-200mg once daily (extended-release) or 12.5-100mg twice daily (tartrate)
Key Monitoring:
- Heart rate: Hold if <50 bpm (alert prescriber)
- Blood pressure: Hold if systolic <90 mmHg
- Symptoms: Fatigue, cold extremities, sexual dysfunction, depression

Critical Interactions:
- Amiodarone + Metoprolol: Additive bradycardia + AV block risk — monitor HR closely
- Verapamil/Diltiazem + Metoprolol: AVOID combination — severe bradycardia risk
- NSAIDs (ibuprofen): May blunt antihypertensive effect

Patient Warning Signs: Sudden increase in fatigue, dizziness on standing (orthostatic hypotension), HR persistently <50

LISINOPRIL (ACE Inhibitor)
Mechanism: Prevents cardiac remodeling; reduces afterload; essential if EF <40%
Standard Dose: 2.5-40mg once daily
Key Monitoring:
- Potassium: Risk of hyperkalemia, especially with spironolactone and CKD — check at 1-2 weeks
- Creatinine: May rise 10-20% initially — acceptable; >30% rise requires dose reduction
- Cough: Dry persistent cough in 10-15% of patients; switch to ARB if intolerable

Critical Interactions:
- Spironolactone/Eplerenone + ACE inhibitor: Hyperkalemia risk — K+ monitoring essential
- NSAIDs: Reduce effectiveness, increase nephrotoxicity risk
- Potassium supplements: Additive hyperkalemia

Do Not Use With: Bilateral renal artery stenosis, prior angioedema with ACE inhibitor, pregnancy

WARFARIN (Vitamin K Antagonist)
Mechanism: Prevents thromboembolism in AF, mechanical valves, and VTE
Target INR: 2.0-3.0 (AF/VTE); 2.5-3.5 (mechanical mitral valve)
Key Monitoring:
- INR: Weekly until stable, then monthly
- Diet consistency: Vitamin K-containing foods (leafy greens) affect INR — consistency more important than avoidance

Critical Interactions (INR RAISING — bleeding risk):
- Amiodarone: Potent warfarin potentiator — reduce warfarin dose by 30-50% when starting amiodarone
- Antibiotics (azithromycin, fluoroquinolones): May raise INR significantly
- Aspirin + Warfarin: Increased bleeding risk — monitor for GI bleeding signs

Critical Interactions (INR LOWERING — clot risk):
- Rifampin, carbamazepine: Major inducers — avoid if possible
- St. John's Wort: Significant INR reduction — avoid

Patient Warning Signs: Unusual bruising, blood in urine/stool, nosebleed lasting >10 min, unusual headache

AMIODARONE (Class III Antiarrhythmic)
Mechanism: Broad antiarrhythmic — used for AF rate/rhythm control and VT prevention
Dose: 200mg twice daily maintenance (loading typically done inpatient)
Key Monitoring:
- Thyroid function (TSH) every 6 months — hyper and hypothyroidism risk
- Pulmonary function annually — pulmonary toxicity (rare but serious)
- Liver enzymes at baseline and every 6 months
- Ophthalmology annually (corneal microdeposits — usually asymptomatic)
- QTc interval monitoring — can prolong QT → Torsades risk

Critical Interactions:
- Warfarin + Amiodarone: Major — amiodarone inhibits warfarin metabolism; reduce warfarin dose 30-50%
- Digoxin + Amiodarone: Digoxin levels double — reduce digoxin dose by 50%
- Statins + Amiodarone: Myopathy risk — avoid simvastatin >20mg; prefer rosuvastatin/pravastatin

FUROSEMIDE (Loop Diuretic)
Mechanism: Reduces fluid overload; essential for heart failure, post-op edema
Standard Dose: 20-80mg once or twice daily
Key Monitoring:
- Daily weights — weight gain >2 lbs overnight or 5 lbs in 5 days → call prescriber
- Potassium: Hypokalemia risk — supplement often required
- Creatinine: Volume depletion can worsen kidney function

Patient Warning Signs: Dizziness on standing, muscle cramps (hypokalemia), excessive thirst, dark urine (dehydration)

SACUBITRIL/VALSARTAN (ARNI — Entresto)
Mechanism: Combined neprilysin inhibitor + ARB; significantly reduces HF mortality in HFrEF
Standard Dose: 24/26mg to 97/103mg twice daily (titrate from lowest dose)
Key Monitoring:
- Similar to ACE inhibitor: Potassium, creatinine, blood pressure
- Never combine with ACE inhibitor (angioedema risk) — 36-hour washout required
- Blood pressure: Hypotension common at initiation

ASPIRIN (Antiplatelet)
Post-CABG: Indefinite aspirin 81mg/day — improves 10-year graft patency
Do NOT stop unless surgeon/cardiologist instructs — risk of in-stent thrombosis

MEDICATION TIMING PEARLS:
- ACE inhibitors and ARBs: Take at consistent time; if causing cough, take at bedtime
- Beta-blockers: Take with food; do NOT abruptly discontinue (rebound hypertension/tachycardia)
- Warfarin: Consistent time daily (evening preferred for INR monitoring)
- Statins: Evening preferred (cholesterol synthesis peaks overnight)
- Diuretics: Morning dosing to avoid nocturia
