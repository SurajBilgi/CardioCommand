POST-CABG MONITORING GUIDELINES — Clinical Reference

CORONARY ARTERY BYPASS GRAFTING (CABG): POST-OPERATIVE CARE STANDARDS

EXPECTED VITAL SIGNS BY RECOVERY PHASE

Days 1-3 (Acute Phase):
- Heart Rate: 60-100 bpm acceptable; transient tachycardia (100-120) may occur due to pain and surgical stress
- Blood Pressure: Target systolic 90-130 mmHg; hypotension (<90) or hypertension (>160) requires immediate assessment
- SpO2: Maintain >94%; supplemental oxygen typically weaned by Day 2
- Respiratory Rate: 12-20 breaths/min; tachypnea >24 requires pulmonary assessment
- Temperature: Low-grade fever <38.5°C (101.3°F) is common in first 72 hours (surgical inflammatory response)

Days 4-7 (Early Recovery):
- Heart Rate: Should trend toward 60-85 bpm on beta-blockade
- HRV (Heart Rate Variability): Normal range 40-80ms; values <20ms indicate autonomic stress
- SpO2: Should be consistently >95% on room air
- Activity: Ambulation 3-4 times daily; target 1,500-2,500 steps

Days 8-14 (Intermediate Recovery):
- Heart Rate: Well-controlled on metoprolol; persistent tachycardia >100 warrants evaluation
- Ejection Fraction monitoring: Patients with EF <40% require closer surveillance for HF decompensation
- Wound healing: Sternal wound should show no dehiscence, erythema, or drainage

Days 15-30 (Late Recovery):
- Activity progression: Target 3,000-5,000 steps/day; cardiac rehab referral
- Medications: Full compliance critical; metoprolol, ACE inhibitor, aspirin, statin cornerstone regimen

SIGNS OF GRAFT FAILURE (URGENT):
- New or worsening chest pain (anginal quality)
- ST-segment changes on remote monitoring
- Sudden hemodynamic instability
- New wall motion abnormalities

ATRIAL FIBRILLATION POST-CABG:
- Incidence: 20-40% within first 5 days post-CABG
- Peak occurrence: Days 2-5
- Risk factors: Age >65, pre-existing AF, poor atrial function, inflammatory state
- Management: Rate control (metoprolol/amiodarone), anticoagulation if AF persists >48 hours
- Monitoring: Continuous telemetry recommended for first 7 days

EJECTION FRACTION THRESHOLDS:
- EF >55%: Normal — standard monitoring protocol
- EF 40-55%: Mild dysfunction — enhanced monitoring, ACE inhibitor essential
- EF 30-40%: Moderate dysfunction — biweekly outreach, fluid monitoring, sodium restriction
- EF <30%: Severe dysfunction — weekly clinical contact, low threshold for readmission

READMISSION RISK STRATIFICATION:
High-risk patients (readmission probability >25%):
- EF <35% + diabetes + age >65
- Prior heart failure hospitalization
- Poor social support/living alone
- Medication non-adherence history
- CKD (GFR <45)
